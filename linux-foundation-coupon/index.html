Which certification is actually worth it?

I get this question at almost every meetup, so here's the advice I actually give, not the brochure version:

  • Completely new to cloud-native? Start with KCNA. It's multiple-choice, it's the cheap one, and it forces you to learn the landscape's vocabulary before you touch a cluster in anger.
  • Working in ops, SRE, or platform engineering? Go straight for the CKA. It's a live-terminal exam, not multiple choice, hiring managers respect it, and the prep transfers directly to your day job.
  • A developer who ships to Kubernetes? CKAD covers pods, deployments, config, probes and debugging. Everything you need, none of the cluster-administration depth you won't use.
  • Already have the CKA? CKS is the natural next step and one of the strongest security signals you can put on a DevOps CV right now.
  • Shaky on Linux itself? Do LFCS first. Kubernetes problems are very often just Linux problems wearing a trench coat.

Don't buy prep until you've used the free stuff

Buy the exam with the discount, sure, but exhaust the free material before paying for prep. I maintain free exam-prep repositories on GitHub (AWS SAA-C03, Azure AZ-104) and write hands-on Kubernetes and observability guides on my blog. For CKA/CKAD/CKS specifically: practice in a real terminal daily. Those exams are speed tests as much as knowledge tests, and no amount of video-watching substitutes for muscle memory.
